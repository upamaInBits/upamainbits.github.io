Human-in-the-Loop ML Ethics Literature Review

• Machine Learning • AI Ethics • Research • Human-in-the-Loop Systems

This project is a literature review exploring ethical considerations in Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) machine learning systems, focusing on how human input influences model behavior, decision-making, and accountability.

The study examines key themes such as fairness, transparency, bias mitigation, and human oversight in machine learning workflows. Human-in-the-loop approaches leverage human judgment to improve model performance and ethical alignment, especially in complex or high-risk scenarios.

This work synthesizes research across multiple papers to identify challenges in integrating human feedback into ML systems, including scalability, subjectivity, and the balance between automation and human control.

The project highlights the importance of designing ML systems that are not only technically effective but also ethically responsible, aligning with broader principles such as transparency, accountability, and fairness in AI systems.

Skills demonstrated: Research analysis, literature synthesis, critical thinking, ethical evaluation of AI systems

XR Reading Usability Study

• XR • Usability • HCI Research • User Studies

This project is a comparative usability study to evaluate how children interact with and comprehend reading content across Virtual Reality (VR), Augmented Reality (AR), and traditional web platforms.

The study involved children aged 6-11 years oldand focused on measuring usability, cognitive load, and reading comprehension when interacting with long-form content such as stories and privacy policies (EULAs).

Data was collected using NASA-TLX, surveys, observational notes, and post-task interviews to analyze user experience across different environments. The findings highlighted differences in immersion, usability challenges, and comfort levels between VR, AR, and web-based reading.

This project emphasizes user-centered evaluation and contributes insights into designing more accessible and effective reading experiences in XR environments.

Skills demonstrated: Usability testing, research design, data collection, user analysis, HCI evaluation

Amazon Review Sentiment Analysis (NLP & Machine Learning)

• Python • Pandas • Scikit-learn • TensorFlow • HuggingFace Transformers • NLTK

Word cloud visualization of the most frequent words in reviews.

This project performs sentiment analysis on Amazon unlocked mobile phone reviews to classify customer feedback as positive, neutral, or negative. The dataset includes over 400k reviews collected from Kaggle and contains textual review data along with ratings and product metadata.

Text preprocessing included tokenization, stopword removal, text normalization, and TF-IDF vectorization. Exploratory data analysis was performed using word clouds, n-gram analysis, and rating distributions to identify patterns in customer feedback.

Multiple models were trained and compared, including Logistic Regression, Random Forest, LSTM, DistilBERT, and ELECTRA. Transformer-based models demonstrated the highest performance in sentiment classification tasks.

Best Model: ELECTRA (~95% accuracy, highest F1-score)
